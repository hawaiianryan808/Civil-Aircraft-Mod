The Boeing 737-700 was the first variant of the Next Generation (NG) series to be delivered, entering service with Southwest Airlines in 1997. It was designed to replace the 737-300, offering greater range, higher speed, and lower maintenance costs. The -700 features a new wing with greater span and chord, and is often equipped with substantial blended winglets. It became the basis for the Boeing Business Jet (BBJ1) and the military C-40 Clipper. Known for its "hot and high" performance, the -700 can operate out of airports with shorter runways than the larger -800.
